Can we swim in Monaco?

Swimming is unsupervised, and the water becomes deep quickly, from the edge of the beach onwards. The area where swimmers can find a foothold is therefore reduced. Also, there are rocks nearby, so swimming is not advised when the sea is rough.

What is Larvotto beach made of?

Larvotto is the principality of Monaco’s most popular sandy beach and the main one with free public access. Covered in smooth imported fine gravel this man-made beach is made up of both private and public areas of beach.

What food is Monaco famous for?

Barbagiuan (crispy and fried pastry)

The Barbagiuan is the most famed dish in Monaco, often being hailed as Monaco’s national dish. This crispy pastry is filled with Swiss chard, and ricotta, before being rolled in pastry and fried until golden crisp with the filling oozing out.

Do you need passport for Monaco?

You must hold a valid passport to enter Monaco. Your passport must be valid for the proposed duration of your stay. No additional period of validity beyond this is required.
