Who was Gary Lautens?

Gary Lautens was a celebrated Canadian humorist and newspaper columnist, known for his work with the Toronto Star from 1962 until his death in 1992.

What awards did he win during his career?

Lautens was awarded a National Newspaper Award in 1963 and won the Stephen Leacock Medal for Humour twice, in 1981 and 1984.

Did he have any involvement in television or radio?

Yes, he was a staff writer for many seasons of the CBC series Front Page Challenge and appeared as a panelist on It’s Your Choice for two seasons. He also hosted radio shows in the early ’60s, interviewing famous entertainers and newsmakers.

What is he known for in his columns?
